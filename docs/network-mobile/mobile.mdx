While many existing mobile networks are built top-down by service providers (e.g., AT&T and Verizon
in the United States) future networks can be a hybrid of large-scale operators and people-owned 5G
Hotspots.

Consumer behavior requires dense networks with more nodes to supply higher bandwidth and lower
latency connections, increasing site acquisition costs.

The Helium model decentralizes these costs and enables communities to participate in building
reliable, high-bandwidth networks using technologies like Citizens Broadband Radio
Service<sup>(</sup>[^1]<sup>)</sup> ("CBRS") and Wi-Fi.

[^1]:
    You can find more information about CBRS on the
    [Federal Communications Commission Website](https://www.fcc.gov/35-ghz-band-overview)
